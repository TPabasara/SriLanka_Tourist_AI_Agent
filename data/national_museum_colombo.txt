Overview and Description: The National Museum of Colombo is the largest museum in Sri Lanka. 
                          It is housed in a grand, gleaming white Italianate-style colonial building. 
                          The museum is the premier institution dedicated to the island's rich cultural and natural heritage, offering a captivating walk through Sri Lanka's millennia-spanning history. 
                          Historical and Cultural Significance: The museum was established on January 1, 1877, by Sir William Henry Gregory, the British Governor of Ceylon at the time. 
                          The impressive building itself, designed by James G. Smither, is a historical landmark. 
                          Main Attractions and Activities: Royal Regalia: The main highlight is the collection of regalia from the Kandyan Monarchs, including the throne and crown of the last King of Kandy. 
                          Historical Galleries: The ground floor galleries are arranged in historical sequence, taking visitors from prehistory through the Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, and Kandy periods. 
                          Cultural Galleries: The upper galleries are arranged thematically, displaying artifacts like traditional masks, puppets, and antique weaponry. 
                          National Museum of Natural History: Located in a separate building on the same premises, this museum houses exhibits on the island's biodiversity, including prehistoric fossils and a two-headed goat. 
                          National Museum Library: Houses a priceless collection of rare books and ancient palm-leaf manuscripts.
